Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 24, 2026

JanNova Elements (“we”, “us”, “our”) respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect personal information when you visit jannova.in (the “Site”), subscribe to our newsletter, or communicate with us about our corporate training, organisation development, HR consultancy, talent management, and coaching services.

By using the Site or providing your information to us, you acknowledge this Policy. Where consent is required by law, we will obtain it before processing your data for that purpose.

1. Who we are

JanNova Elements is the data controller for personal information described in this Policy. We operate in India, the United Arab Emirates, and Qatar.

For privacy enquiries or to exercise your rights, contact us at hr@jannova.in or dinesh@jannova.in.

2. Scope

This Policy applies to information collected through the Site and direct channels linked from it (such as email, telephone, and WhatsApp). It does not apply to third-party websites or platforms you reach via links on the Site; those services have their own privacy policies.

If you engage us under a separate client or service agreement, additional confidentiality or data-handling terms in that agreement may also apply.

3. Information we collect

Depending on how you interact with us, we may collect:

  • Contact details — such as your name, job title, organisation, email address, and telephone number.
  • Communications — the content of messages you send by email, WhatsApp, or other channels, and our responses.
  • Newsletter data — name and email address when you subscribe, plus the fact that you gave consent.
  • Technical data — limited information that your browser or device automatically sends when you load pages (for example, IP address, browser type, and referring URL), typically processed by our hosting or content-delivery provider in server logs.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data (such as health, biometric, or government ID data) through the Site. Please do not send such information unless we have agreed a secure process in writing.

4. How we collect information

  • Directly from you when you complete a form, subscribe to updates, or contact us.
  • Through third-party tools we use to operate the Site (for example, newsletter signup forms hosted by a cloud form provider).
  • Automatically through standard web-server logs when you visit the Site.

5. How we use your information

We use personal information to:

  • respond to enquiries and provide information about our services;
  • prepare proposals, deliver programmes, and manage client relationships;
  • send newsletter and marketing communications where you have opted in;
  • maintain the security and performance of the Site;
  • comply with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations; and
  • establish, exercise, or defend legal claims where necessary.

We process information on the basis of your consent (for example, newsletter signup), performance of a contract or steps prior to entering a contract, our legitimate interests in operating and promoting our business (balanced against your rights), and compliance with legal obligations.

We do not sell your personal data. We do not use third-party advertising or behavioural tracking cookies on this Site.

6. Newsletter and marketing

If you subscribe on the Site, you will be asked to confirm that you agree to receive occasional email updates. We store subscriber details using a third-party online form service (currently Google Forms, linked to a spreadsheet we control) so we can manage our mailing list.

Marketing emails are sent from our business addresses (for example, dinesh@jannova.in or hr@jannova.in). You may withdraw consent at any time by using an unsubscribe link in a message (where provided), replying with “unsubscribe”, or emailing us. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

7. Sharing and disclosure

We may share personal information with:

  • Service providers who assist us under contract (for example, hosting, email, form, or spreadsheet providers), only as needed to perform their services and subject to appropriate safeguards;
  • Professional advisers such as lawyers or accountants, where required;
  • Authorities if required by law, court order, or to protect rights, safety, or property; and
  • Successors in connection with a merger, acquisition, or restructuring, with notice where practicable.

When you use LinkedIn, Instagram, WhatsApp, or similar platforms via links on the Site, those providers process your data under their own terms.

8. International transfers

We are based in India and work with clients in India, the UAE, Qatar, and other regions. Your information may be stored or processed in India or in countries where our service providers operate (including the United States, where major cloud providers may host data). Where required, we take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards for cross-border transfers.

9. Data retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Policy, including to fulfil contracts, resolve disputes, and meet legal, tax, or accounting requirements. Enquiry and newsletter records are typically retained while the relationship is active and for a reasonable period thereafter, unless you request deletion and we are not required to keep the data.

10. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal information against unauthorised access, loss, or misuse. No method of transmission over the internet is completely secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

11. Your rights

Depending on your location and applicable law (including India’s Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, where it applies to you), you may have the right to:

  • access and obtain a copy of your personal data;
  • correct inaccurate or incomplete data;
  • request erasure or restriction of processing in certain circumstances;
  • withdraw consent where processing is consent-based;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interests; and
  • lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority where available.

To exercise these rights, email hr@jannova.in. We may need to verify your identity before responding. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law.

12. Children

The Site and our services are directed at business and professional users. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If you believe we have received such information, please contact us so we can delete it.

13. Cookies and similar technologies

This Site is largely static and does not set first-party analytics, advertising, or profiling cookies. Your browser may store standard session or preference data. Third-party services linked from the Site (such as form providers or social networks) may use their own cookies or similar technologies when you interact with them.

14. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date will reflect the latest version. Material changes will be posted on this page. We encourage you to review this Policy periodically.
